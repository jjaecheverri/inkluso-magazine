The Problem · Our Response
Why calibration exists

The volume of AI-assisted intelligence output has grown faster than any institutional capacity to evaluate it. Market analyses, policy briefs, research summaries, and editorial intelligence pieces — all are now routinely produced with large language model assistance. The prose is polished. The confidence is absolute. The evidence trail, in most cases, is invisible.

This is not a technology failure. It is a structural gap. Publishing pipelines were not designed for content that can simultaneously be fluent and fabricated. Editors working under time pressure do not have systematic tools to separate well-sourced claims from confident hallucination. Readers have no signal at all.

The result is an epistemological infrastructure problem. The volume of produced intelligence is growing. The shared standards for evaluating that intelligence are not keeping pace. Without a calibration layer, readers, institutions, and downstream decision-makers are operating on a trust assumption that the pipeline no longer justifies.

HumanKlu™ is the calibration layer. It is not a fact-checking service. It does not replace editorial judgment. It provides a consistent, human-applied scoring framework — the HumanKlu Calibration Protocol (HKP) — that evaluates AI-generated and AI-assisted content across five structural dimensions: evidence integrity, mechanism clarity, incentive transparency, risk realism, and specificity.

Every certified article carries a public evidence log, a structured HCI score, and a verifiable badge. The record is permanent. The score is human-signed. The standard is public and versioned.

Confirmation is the moat. The organizations that build a public trail of verifiable intelligence will hold a structural advantage over those who scale without one. HumanKlu™ is how you build that trail.

The Standard
HumanKlu Calibration Protocol

Five dimensions. One index. Every score applied by a credentialed human analyst — never automated.

Code Dimension What the Analyst Evaluates Score
EVID Evidence Integrity Are claims backed by identifiable, verifiable sources cited with sufficient specificity to be checked independently? 0–10
MECH Mechanism Clarity Is the causal or logical pathway from evidence to conclusion explained? Are intermediate steps named, not implied? 0–10
INC Incentive Decode Are the incentives of key actors identified and disclosed? Is the author's own position acknowledged where relevant? 0–10
RISK Risk Realism Are contrary evidence and downside scenarios represented with appropriate weight? Is uncertainty acknowledged? 0–10
SPEC Specificity Are claims sufficiently specific to be falsifiable? Are time horizons, magnitudes, and conditions named explicitly? 0–10
HCI = ( EVID + MECH + INC + RISK + SPEC ) ÷ 5
Why These Five?

Each dimension addresses a specific failure mode in AI-assisted writing. EVID targets hallucinated citations. MECH catches plausible-sounding but unverified causal logic. INC exposes undisclosed conflicts. RISK catches omission bias. SPEC targets unfalsifiable vagueness — the hallmark of content that sounds precise but commits to nothing.

The Hard Rule

An article scoring EVID < 6 or where active hallucination is detected receives a REJECTED designation regardless of other scores. No badge is issued. The record is permanently logged in the public ledger.

Fully Human

The HCI score is never calculated from AI output. Every dimension is applied by a credentialed HumanKlu analyst who has passed the HKP Certification Exam and submitted a calibration portfolio. Automation is not permitted in the scoring loop.

07
Governance
Built to be auditable

HumanKlu™ is governed by the HumanKlu Standards Board — an independent committee that ratifies Institutional certifications, adjudicates revocations, and approves protocol updates.

Public Ledger

Every certification, revocation, and expiry is recorded in an append-only public ledger with hash-chained integrity. Nothing disappears. Everything is timestamped.

Open Protocol

The HKP scoring methodology is publicly documented and versioned at hkp-v1. Methodology changes require a 6-month transition period before taking effect.

Revocation Policy

Certifications can be revoked for material alterations, misrepresentation, or hallucination findings post-publication. Revocations are public, permanent, and listed in the ledger unless formally overturned on appeal.

Analyst Independence

Analysts score independently. Payment from creators contingent on score outcome is a permanent ban offense under the Analyst Code of Conduct, enforced by the Standards Board.
