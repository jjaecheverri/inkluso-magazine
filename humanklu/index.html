What We Do

The Problem

As AI authorship scales, the volume of intelligence-grade content — market analysis, policy briefs, research summaries — is growing faster than the institutions designed to evaluate it. AI systems produce fluent, confident prose. Fluency is not accuracy. Confidence is not evidence.

Readers, editors, and downstream decision-makers have no standard by which to assess whether a piece of AI-generated intelligence is structurally sound.

The HumanKlu Response

HumanKlu™ provides a consistent, human-applied scoring framework — the HumanKlu Calibration Protocol — that evaluates AI-generated content across five structural dimensions: evidence integrity, mechanism clarity, incentive transparency, risk realism, and specificity.

Every certified article carries a public evidence log, a structured score, and a verifiable badge — so readers know exactly what has been assessed and to what standard.

The Standard

The HumanKlu Calibration Protocol

Five dimensions. One index. Every score applied by a credentialed human analyst — never automated.

Code Dimension What the Analyst Evaluates Score
EVID Evidence Integrity Are claims backed by identifiable, verifiable sources cited with sufficient specificity to be checked independently? 0 – 10
MECH Mechanism Clarity Is the causal or logical pathway from evidence to conclusion explained? Are intermediate steps named, not implied? 0 – 10
INC Incentive Decode Are the incentives of key actors identified and disclosed? Is the author's own position acknowledged where relevant? 0 – 10
RISK Risk Realism Are contrary evidence and downside scenarios represented with appropriate weight? Is uncertainty acknowledged? 0 – 10
SPEC Specificity Are claims sufficiently specific to be falsifiable? Are time horizons, magnitudes, and conditions named explicitly? 0 – 10
HCI = ( EVID + MECH + INC + RISK + SPEC ) ÷ 5

Full methodology: docs.inkluso.com/humanklu/hkp-v1

Certification Levels

Four tiers. One standard.

Certification level is assigned per article, not per publisher. Each level has a defined HCI threshold, analyst requirement, and renewal cadence.

HK-REVIEWED
HCI 5.5 – 6.9

Standard review. Content has been assessed against HKP criteria by a registered analyst. Structural gaps may be noted.

Renewal: Annual

HK-VERIFIED
HCI ≥ 7.0 · EVID ≥ 7

The standard trust mark. Evidence is predominantly sourced and mechanisms are explained. Suitable for editorial and policy use.

Renewal: Annual

HK-PRO
HCI ≥ 8.0

Professional-grade. Scored by a certified HK-PRO analyst. For institutional reports and professional intelligence products.

Renewal: 6 months

HK-INSTITUTIONAL
HCI ≥ 8.5 · Board Audit

Highest tier. Full audit trail. Lead Analyst and Governance Board sign-off. For regulated or institutional distributions.

Renewal: Quarterly

Articles scoring EVID < 6 or where hallucination is detected receive a REJECTED designation. No badge is issued. Record is logged publicly.
