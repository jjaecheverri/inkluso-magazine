## METHODOLOGY INTRO: SIGNAL VS NOISE

We see something. It's real. Everyone agrees it's real. Then it becomes so obvious that being right about it means nothing.

This is how signals die.

The Lunar New Year retail surge started as a structural observation: migration patterns during the lunar calendar create measurable retail uplift. Real signal. Real money. Real behavior change.

Then it became a prediction hack. Marketing teams bought Lunar New Year playbooks. Retailers expected the surge automatically. Media covered it like clockwork. The signal flattened into noise — so well-known that it stopped revealing anything about *why* capital moves.

But here's what actually happened: The structural shift never stopped. The noise just got too loud to hear it underneath.

This edition explains how we separate the two, using Lunar New Year as the live case study. We're showing you exactly how we think — and proving it with tools you can test yourself.

## PART I: EPISTEMOLOGY
### What Made Lunar New Year Real Signal?

**The knowable part:**
- Migration data by ZIP code (US Census, migration.gov, census tract filings)
- Retail footfall patterns (foot traffic analytics, parking lot data, credit card spend by region)
- Inventory changes (MLS, commercial real estate filings, warehouse permits)
- Search behavior (Google Trends velocity, search intent clustering)

**The verification:**
Three independent structural indicators aligned *before* the surge happened:
1. January census data showed Lunar New Year demographic concentration spike
2. Credit card transaction velocity in high-concentration regions shifted
3. Google search volume for Lunar New Year events preceded retail spending by 7-10 days

None of these required prediction. They were observable *as they happened*.

**The epistemological rule we use:**
- Primary sources > secondary sources (always)
- Correlation at scale > anecdotal observation
- Structural alignment > single-channel signal (wait for 2+ independent indicators)
- Temporal markers matter (dates, timing, causality sequence)

**Why most retail analysis fails here:**
They see the correlation (Lunar New Year → sales up) but don't verify the *mechanism* (migration + calendar + inventory positioning). They turn observation into folklore. Then when the structural conditions shift slightly, they get blindsided.

## PART II: SYSTEMS
### Why Lunar New Year Became Commodity Prediction

**The incentive trap:**
Once a signal becomes known, everyone optimizes for it. Retailers stock extra inventory. Marketing teams launch campaigns. Media covers it yearly. The original signal (structural migration) gets buried under noise (everyone expects it, prices it in, commoditizes it).

**What happens next:**
- Margins compress (everyone's doing it, so competition increases)
- Timing flattens (early-mover advantage disappears; the surge becomes diffuse)
- Narrative wins over observation (media tells the story instead of showing the data)
- False signals multiply (every retail uptick in Feb gets labeled "Lunar New Year effect")

**The system that created the noise:**
1. Consultant finds real signal (migration + retail patterns)
2. Sells playbook to 500 retailers
3. Each retailer implements it, slightly different
4. Signal becomes averaged, diluted, commodified
5. New retailers expect it; old retailers stop paying attention
6. Media covers it as annual certainty, not structural shift
7. Actual structural changes (migration patterns shifting, calendar dates varying) get invisible underneath the noise

**Who profits:**
- Consultants (sold the playbook once)
- Early retailers (moat for 2-3 years)
- Media (predictable annual story)
- Logistics companies (guaranteed seasonal demand)

**Who gets crushed:**
- Late retailers (expensive inventory, competitive compression)
- Immigrant communities (commodified; their movement treated as tradeable data point)
- Market observers (the signal is now buried under so much noise, the next structural shift is invisible)

## PART III: BEHAVIORAL
### Why Retailers Keep Falling for the Same Pattern

**What retailers think they're doing:**
"We're preparing for Lunar New Year demand surge based on historical patterns."

**What they're actually optimizing for:**
- Peer conformity (other retailers are stocking, so we stock)
- Easy narrative (one story explains everything, no thinking required)
- Risk mitigation (excess inventory feels safer than shortage)
- Performance metrics (big seasonal event = predictable headline)

**The behavioral reality:**
Once a pattern becomes *known*, humans stop analyzing it. They stop asking: "Are the structural conditions still true?" Instead, they ask: "Are we executing the known playbook correctly?"

This is where the signal dies.

**Example:** A retailer in a ZIP code where Lunar New Year demographic concentration has *decreased* by 40% since 2023 still stocks extra inventory, because "Lunar New Year surge is a thing." The signal (concentration shift) contradicts the playbook (expect surge). The playbook wins because it's easier.

**The behavioral cost:**
Misaligned inventory. Capital tied up in the wrong place. Missed signals that the demographic base is actually moving somewhere else. A retailer left holding inventory while smarter competitors already shifted supply chains.

## CRUMBS EXPLAINER: HOW WE VERIFY

We use a 6-stage verification standard. Here's what you see, and what stays locked:

### Stage 1: SIGNAL CANDIDATE
*What we do*: Spot the claim ("Lunar New Year drives retail surge")
*How we verify*: Find 2+ independent structural indicators that could align
*What we show you*: The claim + confidence tier (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW based on source count)

### Stage 2: SOURCE TRIANGULATION
*What we do*: Hunt primary sources (census data, filings, permits, official records) + secondary sources (news, academic research, industry reports)
*How we verify*: Score each source on: date, specificity, authority, independence, measurability
*What we show you*: The sources we used, their dates, their domain
*What stays closed*: Our source scoring weights and confidence tiers

### Stage 3: MECHANISM TEST
*What we do*: Ask "what would have to be true for this signal to be real?"
*How we verify*: Build a causal chain (migration → calendar → behavior → retail outcome) and check each link
*What we show you*: The causal chain we found
*What stays closed*: Our confidence scoring in the chain, our weighting of mechanism strength

### Stage 4: HUMAN CONFIRMATION
*What we do*: Run the signal past people *in the system* (retailers, logistics, community members, market analysts)
*How we verify*: Do they recognize the pattern? Do they see the mechanism working?
*What we show you*: That confirmation happened, the date it happened
*What stays closed*: Who confirmed it and what they said (sources stay anonymous unless they agree otherwise)

### Stage 5: ARTIFACT PUBLICATION
*What we do*: Ship the verified analysis (article + evidence + audit)
*How we verify*: HTML report + JSON evidence file + YAML audit trail
*What we show you*: Everything we found, formatted for reading + machine parsing
*What stays closed*: Our internal confidence scoring and future signal indicators

### Stage 6: RESIDUE REVIEW
*What we do*: Wait 30-90 days. Did the signal hold? Did we miss something? Did the system change?
*How we verify*: New data arrives. We compare prediction to reality.
*What we show you*: Updated article with new verification markers
*What stays closed*: How residue data affects our confidence weights
