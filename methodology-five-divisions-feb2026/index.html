Creator Economy Paradox

$250 billion global creator economy. 96% of creators making <$500/month. But top creators building product empires, abandoning platform revenue.

The Noise: "Creator economy is dying."
The Signal: "Creators are leaving platforms and building real businesses."

Epistemology

What makes it knowable?

  • Creator surveys (Influicity 2026)
  • Platform IPO filings (Meta, YouTube)
  • Founder funding for creator tools
  • Exit velocity acceleration Q3–Q4 2025

Systems

Why do incentives create noise?

  • Platforms: Keep creators dependent
  • Consultants: Sell success frameworks
  • Smart creators: Build off-platform
  • Capital flows to exits, not platforms

Behavioral

What are people optimizing for?

  • Creators: Sustainable income + ownership
  • Platforms: Growth + lock-in
  • Investors: Margin + recurring revenue
  • Structural misalignment forces exits

Incentive Realignment in Publishing

Beamly, Substack, and similar platforms pay writers based on quality metrics (return reads, deep engagement), not attention metrics (clicks, time on page).

The Noise: "New platform pays writers better."
The Signal: "Measurement model inversion changes what writers produce."

Epistemology

What makes it knowable?

  • Beamly creator earnings data (Feb 2026)
  • Substack founder essays on revenue
  • Content analysis shift (18-mo cohort)
  • Editorial output measurably different

Systems

Why do incentives create noise?

  • Traditional media: Clicks → ad revenue
  • New platforms: Quality → subscription revenue
  • Writers: Sustainable income + audience trust
  • Measurement change flips all behavior

Behavioral

What are people optimizing for?

  • Click-pay writers: Novelty, outrage, pattern
  • Quality-pay writers: Depth, verification, trust
  • Same person, different incentive structure
  • Behavior changes immediately upon shift

Sunbelt Real Estate Recalibration

At 6.09% mortgage rates (Feb 2026), Sunbelt real estate is fragmenting. Remote work pulled demand there (2020–2022). Now rates are pulling demand away. Mid-sized cities gaining on coastal migration.

The Noise: "Sunbelt exodus."
The Signal: "Rate environment restructures who can afford to live where."

Epistemology

What makes it knowable?

  • Zillow migration reports (Jan 2026)
  • Federal Reserve mortgage rate data
  • Census migration + demographic data
  • Q3 2025: Clear inflection point

Systems

Why do incentives create noise?

  • Investors: Buy appreciating Sunbelt real estate
  • Consultants: Sell relocation services
  • Residents: Affordable + sustainable housing
  • Rate environment kills original signal

Behavioral

What are people optimizing for?

  • Pre-2022: Cheap + remote-capable
  • 2024–2026: Affordable + rate-sustainable
  • Constraint set change = destination change
  • Denver/Austin no longer pencil; Columbus/Des Moines do
