Editorial Standard

Not everything gets published.
Only what scores.

The Kontrarian Scale is our editorial standard. Every piece of content submitted by a Kontributor is scored across five dimensions. To be published, it must meet the threshold. To be featured, it must exceed it.

The Five Dimensions

Every piece is scored on five criteria.
No exceptions.

01
Authenticity
Is this lived experience or borrowed perspective?

We can tell the difference between someone who walked that street at 2am and someone who read about it. Your story must come from a place only you could access. First-hand counts. Second-hand has to be ironclad.

Authenticity88 / 100
Passes "A conversation with the chef who refused to sell his restaurant to a hotel chain"
Fails "Based on what locals say, the neighborhood has changed a lot"
02
Point of View
Does this take a stance?

Neutral is not a virtue here. We don't want "on one hand, on the other hand." Kontrarian content has a perspective — it may invite disagreement, but it is never afraid to land somewhere. Opinion is not the same as bias; one is deliberate, the other is lazy.

Point of View72 / 100
Passes "El Poblado lost its soul the moment it started optimizing for Airbnb reviews"
Fails "El Poblado has changed over the years and different people have different opinions"
03
Craft
Is it worth reading twice?

Sentences matter. Rhythm matters. A great idea buried in flat prose is a missed opportunity. We're not asking for literary fiction — we're asking for writing that respects the reader's time and intelligence.

Craft90 / 100
Passes A lead paragraph that drops you into the scene without explanation
Fails "In this article, I will discuss the current state of the nightlife in Madrid."
04
Cultural Depth
Does it add to the conversation or just describe it?

Anyone can describe. Kontributor content connects — to history, to tension, to what's changing and what refuses to. The best pieces leave you thinking about something beyond what you just read.

Cultural Depth85 / 100
Passes A piece on Medellín street art that traces the political lineage of the murals
Fails "There is a lot of street art in Medellín, and it's very colorful and interesting."
05
Reliability
Can we stand behind this?

Names, places, facts — verify them. Kontrarian is building trust. One sloppy error undermines everything around it. This doesn't mean every piece needs citations — it means you don't publish something you can't defend.

Reliability78 / 100
Passes "The bar opened in 2019 according to the owner, who I interviewed in person"
Fails "The bar has been around for years, or at least that's the impression I got"
