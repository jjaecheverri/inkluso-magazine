About Kontrarian

We are not a magazine.
We are a point of view.

Kontrarian exists because cities are full of stories that never get told — not because no one lived them, but because no one with real access bothered to write them down.

Why Kontrarian

The internet made everyone a publisher. It made almost no one a writer worth reading. The feeds are full of takes — hot, cold, recycled. Opinions dressed as insight. Listicles pretending to be criticism. Somewhere in that noise, the actual story of a city — its bars and its power, its streets and its contradictions — gets buried under content produced by people who've never been there past the airport.

Kontrarian is built on a simple bet: the most interesting content comes from people who are actually embedded in a place. Not visiting. Not remote. There. The kind of person who knows which table at which restaurant is where the decisions get made — and can write about it without making it sound like a press release.

Medellín and Madrid are the first two chapters. More cities will follow — but only when the right Kontributor is found. We'd rather move slowly and stay true than scale fast and become everything we're against.

Ikluso
The parent community

Kontrarian doesn't exist in isolation. It's the editorial voice of Ikluso — a community being built for people who want to live, work, and actually belong in the cities they choose. Not pass through. Not remote-work from a WeWork. Belong.

Ikluso is being built carefully. That means content and trust come first — before anything is sold, before memberships are pitched, before the community is monetized. Kontrarian is how we earn the right to eventually ask for more. It's the layer that proves we're serious.

If Ikluso is the city you choose to inhabit, Kontrarian is the voice that tells you what's actually happening there.

Our Standards

We don't publish everything.
We publish what scores.

Every piece of content on Kontrarian is evaluated against five dimensions. It's not a checklist — it's a standard. The score determines whether something gets published, and the full methodology is available for anyone who wants to understand exactly what we're looking for.
Authenticity Is this lived experience or borrowed perspective?
Point of View Does this take a stance?
Craft Is it worth reading twice?
Cultural Depth Does it add to the conversation or just describe it?
Reliability Can we stand behind this?

The Founder

Juan
Founder & Editor
Medellín · Madrid
"I built this because I was tired of reading about the cities I love from people who'd never been there past the airport."

That's the honest version. The longer version is that I've spent years in Medellín and Madrid, watching both cities get written about in ways that felt completely foreign to the places I actually knew. The rooftop bars. The neighborhoods that don't show up in guides. The people building things quietly. None of it makes it into the content.

I'm not interested in building another platform. I'm interested in building something that earns a place in how people understand the cities they're choosing to live in. Kontrarian is that — slowly, carefully, without shortcuts.

If it resonates with you, you're probably the kind of person we built it for.
