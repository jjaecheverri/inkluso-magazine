The Role, Honestly

A day in the life of a Kontributor

You live there. You notice things. A bar closes and you feel it before you read it anywhere. A new mural goes up and you already know the politics behind it. The neighborhood is different now — not dramatically, but differently — and you're the one who can articulate exactly why.

You're not a reporter. Reporters file dispatches from cities they've just landed in. You're something harder to replace: a cultural translator. The person who can explain what a new bar actually means for the block, not just that it opened. The person who can trace the mood of a street to something that happened three years ago that nobody covered.

You write when you have something worth saying. We ask for a minimum of two pieces per month — not because we want volume, but because we want you to stay sharp, stay present, stay in the city's rhythm. There is no maximum. Some months you'll write four. Some months you'll spend three weeks on one thing. That's fine. Quality is the only rule.

Every piece you submit goes through the Kontrarian Scale. We score it. We tell you what worked and what didn't. If it's close, we work through it together. If it's there, we publish it — under your name, as the authoritative voice for your city.

In the beginning, the compensation is a byline that means something. As Kontrarian's readership builds, your earnings build with it. We've designed this so that the writers who showed up early, who built the trust and the body of work, are the ones who benefit most when the platform scales.

The Kontrarian Scale

What we're looking for.

We don't score on style alone. We score on what actually makes a Kontributor worth reading.

Local Authenticity You can't fake it

Do you actually live there? Can you prove it with what you write? This is the foundation of everything.

Point of View Have an opinion

Kontrarian is not a directory. We think some things are better than others. Your voice should too.

Craft & Clarity Quality, not volume

We'll coach you on this if needed. But you need a foundation of clear, intentional writing.

Cultural Depth Go past the surface

Context. History. The reason something matters. We don't just document — we explain why it counts.

Reliability Show up

One great piece is a tryout. The role requires consistency. We're looking for people who will still be here in two years.
