Buenos Aires

Theatrical, tragic-beautiful, conversation-based.

Candlelight Mozart/Beethoven
Friday, Feb 20
Candlelight in old colonial rooms. Strings and melancholy. The kind of beauty that makes you think about what you've lost.
Candlelight Phil Collins
Friday, Feb 21
Phil Collins played by a string quartet in candlelight. Only in Buenos Aires does this make sense and feel profound.
Noche de Tango
Friday, Feb 20
Live traditional tango. Dancers who learned on the street, not in studios. The kind of movement that comes from heartbreak.
Tango by the River
Saturday, Feb 22
Barrio Norte. Water and music and people who've perfected sadness into art.
Indie Rock Venues
All Weekend
San Telmo. Young people in leather jackets. Electronic music mixed with acoustic. The city's always been indie.
Late Night Conversations
24/7
Wine bars that don't open until 10pm. Argument as a sign of care. This is the real scene.

What It Smells Like

Wine and old stone. Cigarette smoke in theaters. The river smell. Perfume and leather. Coffee at midnight. The smell of a city that invented elegance through pain.
We invented melancholy. We perfected it. Now we can't stop.

New York

Hyper-local, movement-based, always leaving.

Future Tones Festival
Feb 20-23
Czech classical and experimental music. Small rooms full of people who moved here to do something nobody else is doing. The kind of show that changes how you listen.
Femmeland at Sea
Weekend Cruise
Floating festival. DJs, artists, strangers becoming friends for a weekend. The New York move: take it offshore.
Comedy Tours
Multiple Venues
Stand-up that's mean and true. The kind of comedy that comes from arguing with yourself.
Brooklyn Pop-ups
All Weekend
Galleries, restaurants, temporary spaces. Everything here is temporary. That's the point.
Midnight Conversations
24/7
Late-night diners. People arguing about things that don't matter because argument is care. Speed and precision.
Concrete & Coffee
Morning Ritual
Fast everything. Moving. Leaving. The city is only real if you're always about to leave it.

What It Smells Like

Coffee and concrete. Subway air. Perfume and leather jackets. Cold metal. Fast sweat. The smell of a city that's always one step ahead of itself.
We say diverse. We mean: expensive, fast, changing.

Patient, infrastructure-minded, unself-conscious.

All Weekend
Family-scale. Multi-generational. People who chose to be here ten, twenty, thirty years ago. Building something that wasn't obvious.
Bentonville Public Spaces
All Weekend

Emerging Galleries & Food
All Weekend
New shops and makers finding their place. Slow, steady. Not flashy. Real.
Retail Strip Culture
24/7

Nobody told us this was the move. We're just here building it anyway.
