METHODOLOGY: 100 Signals, 5 Deep Dives

How IN-KluSo separates signal from noise through verification discipline

IN-KluSo Intelligence Edition | February 21, 2026 | 90 Minutes Research

How We See Before Consensus Sees

We hunted 100 real signals across five divisions and six cities. Capital flows, company formations, platform shifts, attention migration, territory changes. We ranked them by verifiability, specificity, signal clarity, and non-noise confidence. Then we verified the top five.

What separates signal from noise isn't complexity. It's verification discipline.

The Five-Stage Process:
  1. Hunt: 20 candidates per division using 6+ channels (journalism, permits, court records, real estate data, founder activity, infrastructure signals)
  2. Score: Composite rank by verifiability + specificity + signal clarity + non-noise + recency
  3. Verify: 6-stage CRUMBS standard (candidate → triangulation → mechanism → confirmation → artifact → residue review)
  4. Explain: Three lenses (epistemology, systems, behavior) applied to each signal
  5. Deploy: Interactive tools, confidence scoring, signal genealogy, track through Q2 2026

Epistemology: What Makes This Knowable?

Systems: What Incentives Make This Misaligned?

The uncomfortable truth: This isn't "which app is better." It's which app gives creators the fastest dopamine loop for growth perception. TikTok prioritizes new creator reach; Instagram prioritizes existing creator revenue. Creators optimize for growth signal (for next brand deal), not sustainable income. Both platforms profit from creator churn.

Behavioral: What Are People Actually Optimizing For?

Creators optimize for visible growth + brand deal eligibility (TikTok's algorithm delivers this faster). Instagram optimizes for DAU and revenue from established creators. Misaligned incentives drive migration from revenue-focused to growth-focused platform.

Instagram APAC -18%, TikTok +34%. Creator migration from revenue-platform to growth-platform. Incentive misalignment drives exit.

Epistemology: What Makes This Knowable?

Four independent primary sources: CBRE Q1 report (25.1% vacancy), Newmark data (Class A net absorption negative), LA City Council Feb 9 policy announcement, NY Post commercial coverage. Confidence: HIGH. Real estate data is public, institutional, timestamped.

Systems: What Incentives Make This Transformation Possible?

The uncomfortable truth: This isn't "office market recovery." It's "office space is structurally unrentable, capital is salvaging what remains." Conversion has better risk-adjusted returns than new construction. Municipal incentives cost city revenue but improve political metrics. Landlords prefer salvage terms to extended vacancy.

Behavioral: What Are People Actually Optimizing For?

Developers optimize for available capital + speed to exit. City optimizes for political wins (housing units approved) without addressing affordability. Investors optimize for distressed asset buyups + valuation resets. Narrative: "adaptive reuse Renaissance." Reality: "office apocalypse, salvage operation."

CRUMBS Signal
LA office 25.1% vacancy. Adaptive reuse policy formalizes office-to-residential capital transition. Structural, not cyclical.
